When all this started, and for a long time after, my presence on digital platforms was mostly fueled by a problematic obsession with ideological battles and the spreading of unsolicited opinions. I walked away from all that years ago—roughly the same amount of time it took me to grow from a newborn to a first-grader.

During my recovery, I stumbled upon a hashtag that connected me with something close to the internet’s original utopian promise: a network fundamentally built to spark the flow of knowledge and shared learning. I’m talking about #100DaysOfCode, a kind of bizarre Twitter parallel universe where people actually encouraged each other, helped out, admitted their struggles, and shared solutions.

On April 14, 2022, an entity I’ll call Lex Luthor announced he was taking over the platform where that hashtag (and the community it fostered) lived. The tag is probably still there, but given my track record, I couldn't—or wouldn't—keep wasting my life on that site.

As I post this, I still don’t know if #100DaysOfCode has found the same footing on Bluesky or Mastodon that it had on Lex Luthor’s platform. But I’ve been on Bluesky for over a year now, and after just a few posts, I received a warmer welcome from strangers than I ever expected. Since then, I’ve been stuck—or rather, idling—waiting to figure out how to use the platform without falling back into toxic habits. Honestly, this whole "challenge" thing—this very American trope of announcing a goal to an audience that didn't ask for it—is the only way I feel comfortable, useful, and safe.

So, I’m adding #100DaysOfDH to the original tag. That’s what I’ve been doing anyway for—well, I don’t know if it’s been a hundred days, but it’s been many, and always one day at a time.

Tomorrow we’ll see if it sticks, and whether it turns into a piano solo or a full choir.
